The Free Google Tool Most Houston Businesses Are Ignoring

June 12, 2026 · 5 min read · ← Back to The Briefing

Quick test: pull out your phone and search for what you do plus "near me." Say, "HVAC repair near me" or "taqueria near me." See those three businesses in the map box at the top? That's where the phone calls go. If you're not in there, your competitors are answering calls that should've been yours.

The thing that decides who lands in that box isn't your website, your ad budget, or how long you've been in business. It's mostly your Google Business Profile — a free listing that most Houston small businesses set up once, half-finished, and never touch again.

I review these profiles all the time, and the same five problems show up over and over. Fix them this weekend and you're ahead of most of your local competition.

1. Your Categories Are Wrong (Or Missing)

Google lets you pick one primary category and up to nine more. Most businesses pick one generic category and stop. If you're a plumber who also does water heater installs and drain cleaning, every one of those is a category — and every missing one is a search you don't show up for.

2. You Have Six Photos From 2019

Profiles with regular photo uploads get more clicks and direction requests — Google has said so themselves. You don't need a photographer. Your phone, decent light, and ten minutes a month: the truck, the storefront, the team, the work. Before-and-after shots are gold if you're in trades.

3. You're Not Answering Reviews

Two things matter here. First, review count and recency are ranking signals. Second — and this is the part people miss — your replies are marketing. When a prospect reads your reviews, they're also reading how you respond. A calm, professional reply to a bad review wins more trust than ten five-star ratings. Reply to every single one, good or bad.

4. Your Hours and Phone Number Don't Match the Internet

Google cross-checks your profile against every other place your business appears online — Yelp, Facebook, the BBB, industry directories. If your address is written three different ways or an old phone number is floating around, Google trusts your listing less and ranks it lower. Cleaning this up (it's called "citation consistency") is tedious, unglamorous work, and it moves the needle.

5. You Never Post

Google Business Profiles have a posts feature — short updates with a photo, like a mini social feed. Almost nobody uses it. A post or two a month about a job you finished, a seasonal offer, or a tip for customers tells Google the business is alive and gives searchers one more reason to pick you.

The Honest Truth About Doing This Yourself

None of this is hard. It's just consistent, boring work that's easy to drop when you're busy running the actual business — which is exactly why most profiles go stale, and exactly why keeping yours fresh works so well.

Do it yourself with the checklist above, and you'll see movement in a couple of months. Or if you'd rather have someone own it — the profile, the reviews, the citations, the posts, the monthly report in plain English — that's literally what my Local SEO package is for.
